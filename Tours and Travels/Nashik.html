NASHIK: THE SPIRITUAL & WINE CAPITAL

Trimbakeshwar

Shri Trimbakeshwar Temple, situated approximately 28 km from Nashik, is a significant pilgrimage site in Maharashtra, India. Known for being one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, Trimbakeshwar offers a unique blend of spiritual ambiance, stunning natural beauty, and rich historical significance, making it a must-visit destination for tourists and devotees alike.

Shree Saptshrungi Gad Vani Devi

Located 60 km from Nashik in Kalwan Tahsil, Shree Saptshrungi Gad is a revered pilgrimage site in Maharashtra. This sacred shrine, dedicated to Goddess Saptashrungi Nivasini, is nestled among seven hills near Vani. Visitors to Shree Saptshrungi Gad can experience spiritual tranquility, stunning natural beauty, and exciting trekking opportunities.

Panchvati

Panchavati, situated in Nashik, Maharashtra, holds deep religious and historical significance as per the epic Ramayana. This sacred site is revered as the spot where Lord Rama, accompanied by Sita and Lakshmana, spent a part of their exile. Beyond its spiritual importance, Panchavati offers a blend of cultural heritage and picturesque landscapes, making it a must-visit destination in Nashik.

Kalaram Temple

Kalaram Mandir is located in the Panchvati area of Nashik City, approximately 3 km from the Central Bus Stand. It is easily accessible by city buses and auto-rickshaws from various parts of the city. According to legend, this temple stands at the exact site where Lord Ram resided during his exile.

Sita Gufa

The Sita Gufa, alternatively spelled as Sita Gumpha or Sita Gupha, holds significant importance in the Ramayana, a pivotal epic in Hindu mythology. This cave is a revered pilgrimage site within Panchavati, drawing pilgrims seeking to connect with the legends of the Ramayana.

Harihar Fort

Harihar Fort is located in the Trimbakeshwar Mountain Range of the Western Ghats. Constructed during the Seuna or Yadava Dynasty (9th to 14th century), the fort served a pivotal role in safeguarding trade routes through the Gonda Ghat.

Anjaneri Hills

Anjaneri, renowned as the birthplace of Lord Hanuman and named after his mother Anjani, is nestled within the picturesque Nashik-Trimbakeshwar mountain range. Situated about 20 km from Nashik, Anjaneri fort is revered for its spiritual significance and popular among hiking enthusiasts.

Someshwar Waterfall

Someshwar Waterfalls, found near Gangapur on the outskirts of Nashik, are stunning cascades commonly known as Dudhsagar Waterfalls. A favorite destination for both locals and tourists, this picturesque waterfall emerges from the holy Godavari River, providing a tranquil and scenic getaway in Nasik.

Kapaleshwar Mahadev Temple

The Kapaleshwar Temple, located near Ram Kund in Nashik, is among the region's ancient temples, dating back around six centuries to its establishment in 1763. Initially built and later restored by the Peshwa Dynasty, this historic site is accessible via approximately 50 steps, commanding a picturesque view and showcasing Nashik's vibrant cultural legacy.

Sula Vineyard

Founded in 1999, Sula Wines stands as India's premier winery located amidst the scenic vineyards of Nashik. Celebrated for its pioneering spirit and exceptional standards, Sula has elevated Indian wines to international acclaim. Guests can experience vineyard tours, tastings, and the tranquil charm of this renowned destination.

Coin Museum

Established in 1980 under the Indian Institute of Research in Numismatic Studies, this museum is nestled amidst the scenic Anjaneri hills on a sprawling 505-acre campus. It stands as Asia's sole repository with a vast collection and well-documented history of the Indian currency system, making it a unique destination for enthusiasts and researchers alike.
