AMRAVATI: SACRED SPACES & NATURAL BEAUTY

Sitabuldi Fort

Sitabuldi Fort, a renowned historical landmark in Nagpur, is perched on a hill and offers rich historical significance. Notably, it was the battleground of the Battle of Sitabuldi in 1817. A key feature of the fort is the cell where Mahatma Gandhi was held during his imprisonment. Additionally, the fort houses a war memorial honoring the soldiers who sacrificed their lives in the historic battle.

Deeksha Bhoomi

Deekshabhoomi is a major Buddhist pilgrimage site in India, renowned for its historical and architectural significance. Construction began in 1968 on a four-acre site, including quarters for monks. The impressive stupa, completed in 1978, is the largest stupa in Asia and holds the record as the largest hollow stupa globally. Its unique design features a two-storied, hemispherical structure, each level accommodating up to 5,000 monks. Deekshabhoomi's architectural grandeur and its profound historical importance make it a key destination for Buddhist pilgrims.

Seminary Hill

Seminary Hill, situated approximately 6 km from Nagpur city, is a popular spot for visitors seeking stunning panoramic views of the city. Named after St. Charles Seminary, this charming hill features well-maintained walking paths, making it an ideal location for a serene trek and peaceful relaxation. With its pleasant weather and breathtaking vistas, Seminary Hill provides a refreshing escape and a tranquil experience for all who visit.

Ramtek Fort

This ancient temple complex, located within a historic fortress and believed to date back to the 11th century, is one of Maharashtra's oldest shrines dedicated to Lord Ram. According to legend, during his exile (Van Vas), Lord Ram visited this site, met Sage Agastya, and vowed to defeat the evil kingdom of Ravana based on Sage Agastya's counsel. The premises house multiple temples, including those dedicated to Lord Ram, Lakshmana, Kausalya Devi, Ekadashi Mata, Varaha, and Sage Agastya, among others.

Futala Lake

Futala Lake in Nagpur is a top tourist destination, renowned for its captivating attractions. Spanning over 60 acres, this historic lake boasts vibrant musical fountains, dazzling light shows, and a newly constructed gallery, enhancing its appeal. Each year, Futala Lake attracts thousands of visitors from across Central India. Its expansive open spaces, lush greenery, and various outdoor activities make it especially popular with families and children.

Amba Khori

Amba Khori, a breathtaking waterfall near Nagpur, is a perfect destination for a family picnic or a day out with friends. The impressive cascades of water cascading over lush green rocks create a mesmerizing view. This picturesque spot is accessible year-round, making it a great choice for any season. Additionally, its proximity to the Pench River allows visitors to easily include a visit to the riverbanks in their outing plans.

Raman Science Centre

he Raman Science Centre and Raman Planetarium Complex in Nagpur is a dynamic science center affiliated with Mumbai's Nehru Science Centre. Designed to foster scientific curiosity, it showcases the evolution of science and technology, their industrial applications, and their impact on human welfare through engaging exhibits. Named in honor of the renowned Nobel laureate and Indian physicist, Chandrasekhara Venkata Raman, the center offers an interactive experience for visitors of all ages.

Japanese Rose Garden

The Japanese Rose Garden is a perfect sanctuary for mental and spiritual relaxation, named for its design inspired by Japanese rose cultivation. This beautifully landscaped garden, reflecting Japanese horticultural aesthetics, offers a serene environment that captivates visitors. Its picturesque setting is ideal for capturing stunning photographs and appreciating nature’s splendor in India.

Markanda Mahadev Temple

Markanda Mahadev Temple, also known simply as Markanda, is an ancient shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, situated in Chamorshi near Nagpur, Maharashtra. The expansive temple complex features several historic temples, some of which are in a state of disrepair. Due to its ancient architecture and resemblance to Khajuraho, this site is frequently referred to as "Mini Khajuraho."

Sachee Art Gallery

Sachee Art Gallery, celebrated for its diverse and talented exhibits, also highlights the city's rich cultural heritage. This gallery is a prime destination for experiencing the breadth and skill of contemporary art. Located in a spacious heritage building, Sachee Art Gallery is a must-visit attraction during your stay in Nagpur, India.
