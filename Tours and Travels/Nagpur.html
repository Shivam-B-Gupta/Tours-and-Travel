NAGPUR: THE ORANGE CITY OF INDIA

Ambadevi Temple

Situated in central Amravati, the Ambadevi Temple stands as one of the region's oldest temples. Dedicated to Goddess Ambadevi, an incarnation of Goddess Durga, it holds historical significance. Legends recount Lord Krishna's legendary marriage to Rukmini, believed to have taken place within the sacred precincts of Ambadevi Temple.

Wadali Talav

Wadali Talao, located on Chandur rail road in Amravati, is a renowned reservoir originally built to supply fresh water to the city's residents. Today, it serves as a tranquil retreat for both locals and visitors, offering a serene natural environment away from the bustle of urban life.

Melghat Tiger Reserve

Melghat Tiger Reserve, situated in the Indian state of Maharashtra, lies on the southern extension of the Satpura Hill Range known as Gavilgarh Hill. This reserve is renowned for its diverse wildlife and is a significant habitat for tigers, with its highest point at Vairat (1178 meters above sea level) forming its southwestern boundary.

Bamboo Garden

The Bamboo Garden boasts India's largest bamboo collection and is unique for its extensive variety of species. Adventure sports cater to both adults and children, drawing numerous visitors. A prominent feature is the 20-foot-high skywalk, complemented by a range of exciting adventure activities for tourists.

Upper Wardha Dam

The Wardha Dam serves as a crucial lifeline for Amravati district, providing essential drinking water supply across the region. This earth fill straight gravity dam spans the Wardha River, a key tributary of the Godavari River.

Ekvira Temple

The Shri Ekvira Devi Temple is a prominent religious site in Amravati district, located near the renowned Ambadevi Temple. Constructed in 1660, it is dedicated to Goddess Ekvira Devi, considered an incarnation of Amba Mata. This temple is revered as a symbol of 'Shakti' or divine energy in Indian culture.
