MOST VISITED PLACE IN EAST

Buddha Gaya , Bihar

Bodh Gaya is a Buddhist pilgrimage site in Gaya District of Bihar. Famous for the Mahabodhi Temple, It was here under the Bodhi tree that Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment.It is now one of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites, and a lot of foreign countries, including Japan and China, have helped the Indian government in building facilities for the Buddhist pilgrims.

JAGGANATH MANDIR

The Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha, is a revered Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Jagannath, an incarnation of Vishnu, with his siblings Balabhadra and Subhadra. Known for its massive 65-meter-tall spire and intricate architecture, the temple hosts the famous Rath Yatra festival, drawing millions of Vaishnavism followers worldwide.

Dasam Falls

Dasam Falls, located in Karnataka, is a magnificent royal residence and a prime example of Indo-Saracenic architecture. Known for its grand facade, intricate interiors, and vibrant Durbar Hall, the palace is a symbol of the region's rich cultural heritage.

RAJGIR

Rajgir is town where the air that surrounds its beautiful sceneries carries hints of spirituality and vibrant hues of history with links with both Buddhism and Jainism. Set in a green valley and surrounded by rocky hills, Rajgir is a spiritual town with natural serenity amongst the dense forests, mysterious caves and springs.

DARGILING

Darjeeling, a charming hill station in West Bengal, India, is renowned for its breathtaking views of the Himalayas, including Mount Kanchenjunga, and its lush tea plantations. Key attractions include the sunrise at Tiger Hill, rides on the historic Darjeeling Himalayan Railway ("Toy Train"), and visits to expansive tea estates. The Batasia Loop offers panoramic views, while the Padmaja Naidu Himalayan Zoological Park is home to rare animals like the red panda and snow leopard. Other highlights include the serene Japanese Peace Pagoda, the Himalayan Mountaineering Institute, and the Darjeeling Ropeway, which provides stunning aerial views. With its cool climate, vibrant local culture, and colonial-era charm, Darjeeling is a beloved destination for tourists seeking natural beauty and tranquility.

UDAYGIRI AND KHANDAGIRI CAVES

The Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves, located near Bhubaneswar in Odisha, India, are significant historical and archaeological sites dating back to the 2nd century BCE. These rock-cut caves were carved out during the reign of King Kharavela of the Kalinga dynasty and served as residential blocks for Jain monks. Udayagiri, meaning "Sunrise Hill," houses the famous Rani Gumpha (Queen's Cave) known for its intricate carvings and sculptures. Khandagiri, meaning "Broken Hill," features several caves with beautiful inscriptions and reliefs depicting Jain religious themes. These twin hills offer a fascinating glimpse into ancient Jain architecture and artistry, set against a backdrop of natural beauty and panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

VICTORIA MEMORIAL

The Victoria Memorial in Kolkata, West Bengal, is a grand marble edifice built in memory of Queen Victoria, showcasing British colonial architecture. This iconic landmark is surrounded by lush gardens and features an impressive museum housing a vast collection of artifacts, paintings, and manuscripts from the British era. Visitors can explore the majestic central dome, impressive statues, and galleries that depict the historical and cultural heritage of India. Illuminated at night, the Victoria Memorial offers a stunning visual spectacle, making it a must-visit destination for tourists seeking to experience Kolkata's rich history and architectural grandeur.

KOLKATTA

Kolkata, the capital of West Bengal, India, is a city steeped in rich cultural heritage and history, known for its colonial architecture, vibrant arts scene, and bustling markets. Key attractions include the majestic Victoria Memorial, the iconic Howrah Bridge, and the spiritual Dakshineswar Kali Temple. Visitors can explore the Indian Museum, one of the oldest in the country, and the Marble Palace with its impressive collection of antiques. The lively Park Street is famous for its dining and nightlife, while the serene Belur Math offers a spiritual retreat. Kolkata's unique charm is further highlighted by its literary festivals, traditional music and dance performances, and the grand celebration of Durga Puja, making it a captivating destination for tourists.
