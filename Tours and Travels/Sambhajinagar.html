SAMBHAJINAGAR: HISTORY, ART & SPIRITUAL JOURNEYS

Bibi Ka Maqbara

Bibi Ka Maqbara is a stunning tomb located in Aurangabad, Maharashtra. Built in 1660 by Prince Azam Shah in memory of his mother, Dilras Banu Begum, this beautiful monument reflects the rich Mughal architecture and history.

Ellora Caves

Experience the ancient mystery at the 2 km long Ellora Caves, where magnificent temples were carved from rock. As one of the largest ancient rock-cut cave temples in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Ellora Caves showcase the incredible artistic achievements of ancient India. Discover this awe-inspiring confluence of creativity and history.

Ajanta Caves

Located near Aurangabad, Maharashtra, the Ajanta Caves feature around 30 rock-cut Buddhist monuments dating back to the 2nd century BC. These caves are renowned for their mesmerizing architecture and intricate depictions of Buddha's life. Explore the timeless beauty and spiritual significance of Ajanta Caves.

Chattrapati Shivaji Maharaj Museum

Kalaram Mandir is located in the Panchvati area of Nashik City, just 3 km from the Central Bus Stand. Easily accessible by city buses and auto rickshaws, this temple is believed to be the site where Lord Ram lived during his exile. Discover the spiritual significance and historical importance of Kalaram Mandir in Nashik.

Daulatabad Fort

Daulatabad Fort, located 15 km from Aurangabad, is a majestic 12th-century fortification set amidst lush greenery. Known as one of the 'seven wonders of Maharashtra,' this architectural marvel offers a glimpse into ancient history and grandeur. Explore the impressive Daulatabad Fort on your next visit to Aurangabad.

Salim Ali Lake

Salim Ali Lake, also known as Salim Ali Sarovar, is a serene bird-watching haven located in the heart of Aurangabad, opposite Himayat Bagh near Delhi Gate. This picturesque lake attracts a diverse array of migratory birds, making it a must-visit spot for nature enthusiasts and bird watchers.

Shree Grishneshwar Temple

Grishneshwar Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Ellora, is one of the 12 sacred Jyotirlingas in India. Also known as Ghrneshwar or Dhushmeshwar Temple, this revered site in Aurangabad is dedicated to Lord Shiva and holds great pilgrimage importance. As the smallest and the 12th Jyotirlinga, Grishneshwar Temple is a must-visit for devotees and travelers alike.

Jaikwadi Dam

Jaikwadi Dam, located in Aurangabad district, Maharashtra, spans the Godavari River and irrigates approximately 2.40 lakh hectares of Marathwada. Known as Nathsagar, it is the largest earthen dam in Asia, stretching 60 kilometers in length and 10 kilometers in width. Discover the engineering marvel of Jaikwadi Dam, a vital resource for the region.

Himroo Factory

The Himroo Factory in Aurangabad is renowned for producing the traditional Himroo fabric, located near Zaffar Gate in the old town. This fabric, a blend of cotton and silk, is celebrated for its superior quality and incorporates designs inspired by Ajanta paintings. Alongside Himroo material, visitors can explore a variety of items including Paithani saris and traditional handloom shawls, making it a popular destination for tourists and shoppers alike.
