PUNE: HERITAGE, HILLS & HARMONY

Shivneri

Shivneri Fort, located near Junnar in Pune district, Maharashtra, is an ancient military fortification and the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the founder of the Maratha Empire. Perched on a hilltop overlooking Junnar city, Shivneri Fort offers breathtaking views and a deep dive into India's rich history.

Shaniwar Wada

Shaniwar Wada is a historic site located in Pune, Maharashtra, and is one of the must-visit places in the city. Once the seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire, this iconic structure was constructed in the 18th century and suffered significant damage from a fire in 1828. Despite its ruins, Shaniwar Wada remains a popular tourist attraction, drawing visitors with its rich history and cultural significance.

Dagduseth Halwai Ganpati Temple

Dagdusheth Ganpati Temple, located in Pune, is an iconic shrine dedicated to Lord Ganesh. Renowned for its exquisite architecture and vibrant celebrations, this temple draws thousands of devotees and visitors each year. Experience the rich cultural heritage and spiritual atmosphere as you immerse yourself in the festivities and traditions surrounding this beloved landmark.

Sinhagad Fort

Sinhagad Fort, a historic monument of great importance in Maratha history, was originally named Kondhana Fort. Located atop a rugged hill at an elevation of approximately 1,300 meters, this fort has been the site of many battles and has changed hands multiple times over the centuries. Explore the rich history and significance of Sinhagad Fort as you take in its breathtaking views and heritage.

Harishchandragad

Harishchandragad, situated in the stunning Sahyadri mountains of Maharashtra, India, is a magnificent ancient hill fort that showcases the beauty of history and nature. Towering at an elevation of 4,670 feet, this fort has been a favorite among adventurers and history enthusiasts for centuries.

Lonaval/Khandala

Lonavla and the neighboring Khandala are picturesque twin hill stations located 622 meters (2,041 ft) above sea level in the Sahyadri ranges, which separate the Deccan Plateau from the Konkan coast. Tourism peaks during the monsoon season, attracting visitors to their scenic beauty. The name 'Lonavla' comes from the Sanskrit word 'lonavli,' which refers to the many caves nearby, including the Karla, Bhaja, and Bedsa Caves.

Saras Baug

Saras Baug is a prominent tourist attraction in Pune, India, featuring a sprawling garden that covers around 25 acres. This lush space offers a serene environment filled with vibrant greenery. The garden is named after the 'Sarus' cranes frequently seen in the area and is a favorite destination for both visitors and local residents.

Katraj Jain Temple

Katraj Jain Temple is a notable place of worship that embodies peace and tranquility. Situated on a hillock between the Pune-Satara highway in Katraj, Pune district, Maharashtra, this temple offers a serene environment for reflection and devotion.

Aga Khan Palace

The Aga Khan Palace in Pune stands as a national monument significant to India's freedom struggle. During the Quit India Movement of 1942, Mahatma Gandhi, along with his wife Kasturba and secretary Mahadevbhai Desai, was imprisoned at the palace from August 9, 1942, to May 6, 1944. This historic site serves as a reminder of India's fight for independence.

Raj Machi Fort

Rajmachi is a favored spot for trekking and outdoor adventures in Maharashtra's Sahyadri mountain range. Renowned for its historic forts, vibrant greenery, and stunning panoramic views, Rajmachi offers an unforgettable experience for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Prabalgad Fort

Prabalgad and Kalavantin Durg are two notable forts nestled in Maharashtra's Western Ghats, near the scenic hill station of Panvel. These forts are famous for their historical importance and offer challenging trekking trails, making them a must-visit for adventure enthusiasts and history buffs alike.

Bhimashankar Jyotirling Temple

Bhimashankar, situated in the Sahyadri range of Maharashtra, is a revered pilgrimage destination celebrated for its ancient Shiva temple, recognized as one of India's twelve Jyotirlingas. Nestled amidst verdant landscapes and diverse wildlife, it provides a serene retreat and excellent trekking prospects. Its stunning vistas and profound religious importance attract numerous nature enthusiasts and devotees, making it a sought-after spot for spiritual and leisurely pursuits alike.
